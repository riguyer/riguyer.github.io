Paccheri with spicy Italian sausage

  • 1 lb spicy Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 medium white onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup dry red wine
  • 3 tblsp tomato paste
  • 1 lb dry paccheri pasta, or equivalent amount fresh
  • Olive oil
  • Grated Parmigiano
  • Chopped fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper

The key to this recipe is the quality of the sausage. I get mine at Savenor's in Cambridge. The wine will also make a difference, but not to the same degree. I usually use the Charles Shaw Red Blend from Trader Joe's, which will never be confused for Lafite, but is a remarkably decent wine for such a ridiculously low price.

Heat a few dashes of olive oil in a large pan, and sautee the onions until they are becoming translucent. Then add the sausage to the pan, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon as it cooks. When the sausage is mostly cooked, add the wine and tomato paste and mix well. Let simmer for several minutes, until the wine is cooked down and absorbed. Meanwhile, cook the paccheri until it is al dente. Add a few dashes of pasta water to the sauce, stirring until it begins to develops a thick, shiny texture. Then add the pasta to the sauce and cook for 1-2 more minutes on low heat. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Stir in a bit of grated Parmigiano cheese. Serve warm with chopped basil leaves and a few more dashes of Parmigiano on top.

Cocktails

All cocktail names and recipes are, to the best of my knowledge, original. That said, I usually don't do more than a cursory Google search to see if somebody else has had the same idea. If you think you came up with one of these before me, send me an email. I'm happy to give credit where it is due!

The Upper East Side

  • 1.5 parts apple brandy - I prefer St. George California Reserve
  • 1.5 parts sweet vermouth
  • 0.25 parts Contreau or Grand Marnier
  • Dash Angostura or orange bitters
  • Luxardo cherry

This is a twist on the French Manhattan from David Leibowitz's book, with the cognac replaced by apple brandy. I originally called it The Big Apple, but unsurprisingly there is already a cocktail with that name. So instead I named it after the Uptown Manhattan neighborhood where my parents used to live.

Mix the liquors and bitters and stir well with ice. Strain and serve with the cherry.

The Theodore Joseph

  • 1 part Glenfarclas 12 yr
  • 1 part sweet vermouth
  • 0.25 part Drambouie
  • 0.25 part Nux Alpina
  • Dash walnut bitters
  • Luxardo cherry

Mix all liquors and bitters, stir well with ice. Strain and serve with the cherry and a large ice cube.
